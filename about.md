Otago Orienteering Club is a friendly, volunteer-run club for anyone who loves
being outdoors and using their head as much as their legs. We put on
orienteering events right across Otago, from the parks and hills around Dunedin
to the tussock and goldfields country of Central Otago. Whether you are a
seasoned competitor or you have never touched a control flag in your life, there
is a place for you here.

## New name, same club

We are the club formerly known as **Dunedin Orienteering Club**. Our membership
now stretches well beyond the city, so we have rebranded as **Otago Orienteering
Club** to reflect the whole region we serve. Same people, same welcome, same
love of the sport — a name that finally matches where our members live and where
we run events.

## What is orienteering?

Orienteering is a map sport: you navigate between a series of points (called
controls) marked on a detailed map, choosing your own route and finding them in
order. It is you, a map, and sometimes a compass. You can walk, jog or run — the
challenge is as much about reading the terrain and making smart route choices as
it is about fitness.

It suits just about everyone. Courses range from short and easy, perfect for
families and first-timers, through to long and technical for experienced
navigators. There is no special gear to buy to give it a go, and every course is
against the clock only if you want it to be.

## Our goals and aspirations

We are a community club with big ambitions for the sport in Otago. We aim to:

- **Grow our membership** and introduce more people to orienteering across the region.
- **Welcome beginners and families** with approachable courses and a no-pressure atmosphere.
- **Run quality events** throughout Otago, from Dunedin to Central Otago.
- **Support juniors and schools**, helping young orienteers find their feet in the sport.
- **Foster a strong club community** where members of all ages and abilities feel at home.

## What we run

Across the year the club puts on a full spread of orienteering:

- **Club Classic "OY" events** — our main foot-orienteering series, up to five a
  year across Otago with courses from White through to Long Red.
- **Summer & Schools Series** — Term 1 evening events with a beginner-friendly
  three-course format, feeding into the Otago Schools' Champs.
- **Sprint events** — fast, local Dunedin races with winning times of 15–20 minutes.
- **Rogaines** — score events where you choose which controls to get, typically
  three hours or shorter, including popular night rogaines.
- **Mountain Bike Orienteering (MTBO)** — around Alexandra and Naseby.
- **Ski Orienteering** — at the Snow Farm near Wanaka.

## Where we run events

Our events take place in and around **Dunedin** — city parks, bush reserves,
the harbour and peninsula — and across **wider Otago**, including Central Otago
locations such as Alexandra and the Naseby area. From urban sprints to rugged
forest and open goldfields, Otago offers some of the best and most varied
orienteering terrain in the country.

## Getting involved

The easiest way to start is simply to come to an event. You do not need to be a
member to have a go, and you will find friendly faces happy to explain the
ropes. If you enjoy it, becoming a member gets you cheaper entries and connects
you with the wider club community.
