Rather than duplicate it here, we point you to **Orienteering New Zealand
(ONZ)**, our national body, which maintains an excellent and up-to-date library
of guides, training material, rules and safety resources. The links below take
you straight to the good stuff.

## Getting started

New to the sport? Start here.

- [Get started with orienteering](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/get-started/) — the newcomer hub: find an event, pick a course, no membership needed.
- [Intro to orienteering](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/intro-to-orienteering/) — the basics of what it is and how a course works.
- [Why do orienteering?](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/why-orienteering/) — the benefits and reasons to give it a go.
- [Choose the right course](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/choose-the-right-course/) — the colour and difficulty guide (White, Yellow, Orange, Red).
- [Age and ability classes](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/classes/) — how grades and groupings work.
- [Intro to MTB orienteering](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/intro-to-mtb-orienteering/) — mountain-bike orienteering explained.
- [Intro to rogaining](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/intro-to-rogaining/) — the long-distance navigation format.

## Training &amp; coaching

Want to improve? These are the resources our own members use.

- [Coaching and Training Hub](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/coaching-and-training-hub/) — the main self-training resource: technique, videos and skills by difficulty. Start here.
- [Coaching workbooks](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/coaching-workbooks/) — seven skill-level workbooks (with answer guides) for coaches and self-taught orienteers.
- [Coaching resources](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/coaching-resources/) — material supporting the coaching qualification pathway.
- [List of qualified coaches](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/coaches/) — the directory of ONZ-qualified coaches.
- ["Leg by leg" video playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLbZgP0XNzliR2t5bsvH3wWnW3oD_Xk0wA) — route-choice walkthroughs for all levels.

## Map symbols &amp; rules

Learn to read the map and understand the rules of competition.

- [Simplified ISOM legend](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Legend-ISOM-Common-Symbols.pdf) — common forest-map symbols, beginner-friendly (PDF).
- [Simplified ISSprOM legend](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Legend-ISSprOM-Common-Symbols.pdf) — common sprint-map symbols, beginner-friendly (PDF).
- [Complete ISOM legend](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Legend-ISOM-All-Symbols.pdf) — the full forest-map symbol set (PDF).
- [Complete ISSprOM legend](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Legend-ISSprOM-All-Symbols.pdf) — the full sprint-map symbol set (PDF).
- [Control description guide](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/Maprunner-Control-Descriptions.pdf) — a readable guide to control-description symbols (PDF).
- [Competition rules (index)](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/rules/) — the hub for all discipline rules and IOF references.
- [ONZ policies &amp; procedures](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/policies/) — ONZ policies including the Foot Orienteering rules.

## Juniors &amp; schools

For teachers, parents and young orienteers.

- [Intro to schools orienteering](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/intro-to-schools-orienteering/) — the starting point for teachers and schools.
- [Schools Knowledge Hub](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/resources/schools/) — a curated hub of teaching resources.
- [Schools resources](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/schools-resources/) — educational material for teachers, parents, coaches and students.
- [Kiwi-O Manual](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Kiwi-O-Manual-2014-v2.pdf) — the schools orienteering programme manual (PDF).
- ["GO Orienteering" leaflet](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Leaflet-GO-Orienteering_web_final.pdf) — a fun intro leaflet (PDF).

## Safety

Orienteering is a safe sport, and these resources help keep it that way.

- [Health &amp; Safety / Risk Management](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/health-safety/) — club H&amp;S guidance under the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015.
- [ONZ Health and Safety Policy](https://www.orienteering.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/A3-Health-Safety.pdf) — the one-page policy summary (PDF).
- [Online Accident Reporting form](https://forms.gle/fi1WYaALe7bUkGxa8) — report an incident to ONZ.
